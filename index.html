Wend Yam Donald Davy Ouedraogo

I recently completed my Ph.D. in Computer Science, focusing on computational biology, at Université de Sherbrooke, where I worked in the CoBIUS laboratory. I’ve started a postdoc there and am looking for more postdoctoral opportunities. My expertise encompasses genomics, phylogenetics, and computational biology. I focus on developing mathematical and algorithmic approaches to tackle biological challenges. My research specifically investigates the evolution of alternative splicing and the evolution of various transcripts/CDS across multiple species in the Eukaryotic kingdom. Through computational modeling and analysis, I aim to deepen our understanding of these topics. Additionally, I have a strong interest in web development and algorithmic problem-solving.

Softwares and Applications

(Updated on October 29)

TranscriptDB

A transcript-centric database to study transcript conservation and evolution within Ensembl gene trees. [Link]

SimSpliceEvol2

A tool for simulating the evolution of gene sequences that integrates exon-intron structure evolution and sets of transcript evolution through alternative splicing events. It takes a guide gene tree as input and generates a gene sequence with its transcripts for each node of the tree, from the root to the leaves. [Link]
